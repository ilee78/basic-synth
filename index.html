Mini Minimoog

By Isabelle Lee

I. Description

The Minimoog is an analog synthesizer manufactured by Moog Music in the 1970s and 1980s.
It was the first synthesizer sold in retail stores. I tried to mimic the skeletal components
of a very basic, primitive synthesizer in this assignment, hence the name.

To abide by the assignment's focus on "playability", I implemented a waveform visualization
to provide the user immediate feedback when they manipulated the sound, and simple sliders to
control multiple aspects of sound generation and effects.

III. Tutorial

Press the start button. Hold down a key on your keyboard and toggle the frequency slider first.
Then feel free to tinker with the other sliders to your heart's content.
Move the sound in 3D space according to your mouse position in the panner pad.
Watch as the waveforms change in the analyzer!

IV. Description of Parameters

Frequency: Connected to oscillator, changes pitch to a scaled value.

LFO: Connected to oscillator frequency, creates rhythmic pulse.

Volume: Connected to amplifier gain, changes volume of sound.

Delay: Connected to feedback gain, creates echo and overlapping sounds.

Dry/Wet: Connected to convolver attached to dry/wet gain, bestows the
sound interesting properties taken from this impulse. Minimum position
on slider yields entirely dry effect, maximum position yields entirely wet.

Panner: Takes mouse position coordinates as input, moves the sound in space accordingly.

V. "Factory" Presets

Dry/Wet: 100 (maximum position on slider)
Panner: Near bottom right corner

For observing interesting contrasts between dry and wet.
Key: "d".
Frequency: 10 (1/5 of the slider)
LFO: 100 (maximum position on slider)
Volume: default
Delay: default
Dry/Wet: 0 or 100 (minimum and maximum positions on slider)
Panner: default
